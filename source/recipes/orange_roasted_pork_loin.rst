Orange flavoured roasted pork loin
----------------------------------

:term:`Medium`, :term:`Leftovers`

.. image:: /graphics/orange_roasted_pork_loin.jpg
   :width: 40%
   :align: center

My trick is to use :term:`sweet soybean paste <soybean paste>`, which is sweet, salty and savory.
The orange flavour is one of many possibilities, mustard and lemon would be more normal.

Ingredients
^^^^^^^^^^^

For 4 portions:

* :term:`Pork` loin, *filet mignon*, around 500g
* 1 tablespoon :term:`sweet soybean paste <soybean paste>`, the normal one is fine too
* 3 cloves of :term:`Garlic`
* ½ :term:`Orange`

Steps
^^^^^

Mince the garlic.
Combine it with the juice of half an orange, a good amount of the zest, a bit of salt, and the soybean paste.
Marinate the pork in this mixture for 1 or 2 hours.

Place the juice in a narrow oven tray, take the meat out and dry it a bit with some paper towels, also removing any pieces of garlic.
Use a frying pan to sear it on all sides just long enough develop a crust.

Put it back in the tray and roast at 180°C for 30 minutes.
Let it cool a bit before cutting.

.. tip::
   You can use the leftover liquid to make a pan sauce with butter and shallots, but be sure to adjust the acidity and sweetness.
